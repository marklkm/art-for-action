Art for Action

"Art for Action: Building an Inclusive Future" is an inclusive art exhibition aimed at empowering disabled students to express their perspectives and creativity through art. Held from 3-6 December 2024, at Mheitheal - Printing House Square. Join us in building an inclusive future where art serves as a bridge for advocacy, and change. Online Guide

Art is a universal language that transcends boundaries and brings people together. It has the power to connect diverse communities, celebrate unique perspectives, and foster inclusivity. We believe that art should be accessible to everyone, regardless of background, ability, or circumstance. This guide is about more than just viewing art—it's about experiencing, creating, and being part of a community that values every voice.

Background

This is the fourth year of Trinity's Disability Art Exhibition. The Trinity Provost & President Dr Linda Doyle will offically open the Art for Action: Building an Inclusive Future! Exhibition, on Monday 2nd December at 5:30PM, in An Meitheal, Trinity disAbility Hub, Printing House Square.

In 2023 the exhibition was entitled "Beyond Boundaries: Art Unleashed " and took place in An Mheitheal, Printing House Square.

In 2022 the exhibition took place in An Meitheal, Printing House Square with the theme “UNIQUE: A Disabled Perspective”.

In 2021 the exhibition took place in the Hamilton Zóin Mac Leinn with the theme “Defining Disability”.

This year's exhibition is a collaboration between the Trinity Ability co_op, and the Trinity College Dublin Student's Union (TCDSU). A huge thank you to the Trinity disAbility Service, and Declan Treanor - disAbility Service Director, for their help and support with the exhibition. Our sincerest thanks to all the artists who submitted their extraordinary work!

Robert Smith 1

A thought-provoking illustration showing a broken elevator on a university campus with a student in a wheelchair looking at a sign reading 'Out of Order'.

What does art mean to you?

Art is.... God, that's a big one. To me, art is just something you do and you say it's art.

What role does art play in building an inclusive future?

Art can be anything—and that's what makes it so powerful. It's a space where artists can express every part of themselves, and in that sense, it invites everyone to be whatever they wish within it. Art's ability to embrace all forms and voices allows us to imagine and build a future where everyone belongs, unrestricted by labels or limitations.
